# Constitution Civic Rights Source Notes

Source: Constitution of Kenya.

Article 38 protects political rights. Every adult citizen has the right to be registered as a voter, to vote by secret ballot in any election or referendum, and to be a candidate for public office.

Article 81 sets the general principles for Kenya's electoral system, including free and fair elections, universal suffrage based on the aspiration for fair representation and equality of vote, and voting by secret ballot.

Article 86 requires the electoral body to ensure that voting is simple, accurate, verifiable, secure, accountable, and transparent.
